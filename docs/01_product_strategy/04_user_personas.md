# User Personas: Nomen

To ensure Nomen meets real-world user needs, we design around three target user personas: the Bootstrapped Solopreneur, the Venture-backed Tech Founder, and the Creative Agency Brand Strategist.

---

## 1. Sarah, the Bootstrapped Solopreneur
*“I need a name that sounds professional and has an available domain, without spending weeks or thousands of dollars.”*

### Demographic Details
- **Role**: Freelancer / E-commerce Creator
- **Company size**: 1 (Self)
- **Tech Savviness**: Medium (uses Shopify, Canva, Notion)
- **Budget**: Low ($10 - $100 for domain acquisition)

### Goals & Motivations
- Launch her handmade skincare line quickly.
- Avoid legal issues with existing skincare brands.
- Find a catchy, simple name that has an available `.com` or `.co` domain.

### Pain Points
- Finds that every name she thinks of has an already-taken domain.
- Lacks design skills to create a logo or brand mockups.
- Confused by the process of checking trademarks.

### How Nomen Solves Her Problems
- Nomen's AI generates brandable names with instant TLD domain checks.
- Automatically generates color palettes and logo suggestions so she doesn't have to hire a designer immediately.
- Explains trademark safety ratings in simple, layman terms (Green, Yellow, Red).

---

## 2. Marcus, the Venture-Backed Tech Founder
*“We are building a category-defining AI infrastructure platform. The name must be unique, phonetically strong, and legally bulletproof across global jurisdictions.”*

### Demographic Details
- **Role**: Co-founder & CTO
- **Company size**: 2 - 15 (Early-stage startup)
- **Tech Savviness**: Extremely High
- **Budget**: Medium-High (up to $5,000 for domain acquisition)

### Goals & Motivations
- Select a name that can scale globally from day one.
- Pass legal due diligence for upcoming seed funding.
- Ensure the name is easy to pronounce on podcasts, keynotes, and sales calls.

### Pain Points
- Co-founders have conflicting subjective opinions on name suggestions.
- Worried about receiving Cease & Desist letters after launching.
- Finding similar names in the tech space that could confuse search engines (SEO issues).

### How Nomen Solves His Problems
- Provides the **Brand Score Index (BSI)** to grade candidate names objectively.
- Performs multi-jurisdiction trademark clearance reports across USPTO, UK-IPO, and EUIPO.
- Offers **Semantic Similarity Detection** to ensure their name does not clash with existing tech companies.

---

## 3. Elena, the Agency Brand Director
*“I coordinate naming and positioning workshops for mid-market corporate clients. We need a tool that lets us brainstorm, organize, and export branding portfolios.”*

### Demographic Details
- **Role**: Principal Brand Strategist
- **Company size**: 20 - 50 (Boutique Creative Agency)
- **Tech Savviness**: High (Figma, Adobe Creative Suite)
- **Budget**: High ($500+/month for professional tools)

### Goals & Motivations
- Present curated name lists along with visual identities to clients.
- Speed up the internal brainstorming phase.
- Perform quick domain/trademark checks before showing names to the legal team.

### Pain Points
- Showing client names in plain text on slide decks fails to build excitement.
- Manually compiling logo mockups, colors, and domain options takes hours.
- Checking trademark registries is slow and tedious.

### How Nomen Solves Her Problems
- Let's her save name candidates into collaborative portfolios.
- Generates beautiful mockups (landing pages, mobile screens) dynamically, saving design time.
- Offers one-click **Brand Identity Package exports** containing vectors (SVGs) and palette configurations.
